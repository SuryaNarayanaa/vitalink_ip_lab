# Product

## Register

product

## Users

VitaLink is used by patients managing INR monitoring and medication adherence, doctors reviewing patient reports and dosage plans, and administrators managing hospitals, users, audit logs, configuration, and operations. Users are often in clinical or care-coordination contexts where clarity, trust, and fast recovery from errors matter more than decorative expression.

## Product Purpose

VitaLink helps care teams monitor anticoagulation therapy, collect INR reports, track dosage adherence, communicate doctor updates, and administer a multi-tenant hospital platform. Success means users can complete critical workflows reliably, understand system state quickly, and recover safely from mistakes or service problems.

## Brand Personality

Calm, clinical, dependable. The product should feel steady and professional, with human warmth in the copy and clear feedback when something goes wrong.

## Anti-references

Avoid marketing-style hero layouts inside the app, decorative card-heavy clutter, raw technical exception dumps, vague failure messages, and visual treatments that make clinical status or errors feel casual.

## Design Principles

1. Make system state obvious, especially around authentication, uploads, notifications, and API failures.
2. Use plain clinical language that tells users what happened and what to do next.
3. Preserve trust by distinguishing user-correctable errors from service or configuration problems.
4. Keep repeated workflows dense enough for daily clinical use, but not cramped.
5. Treat accessibility and recovery paths as part of the core workflow, not edge polish.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Aim for WCAG AA contrast and interaction clarity. Error states must not rely on color alone, should work on small mobile screens, and should offer keyboard/screen-reader-readable labels and retry actions where relevant.
